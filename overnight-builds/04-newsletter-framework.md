## Subject Line Formulas (5 Tested Variations)

### Formula 1: QUESTION (Curiosity-Driven)
- "What if YOUR sleep mattered too?"
- "When was the last time you felt like yourself?"
- "Are you making this common sleep mistake?"
- "What if you didn't have to choose?"
- "Ready to stop feeling guilty about rest?"

**Why it works:** Questions engage the brain, create curiosity gap, feel personal

---

### Formula 2: PERMISSION STATEMENT (Emotional Relief)
- "Permission to rest without guilt"
- "You're allowed to need more help"
- "It's okay to prioritize YOUR sleep"
- "No, you're not failing"
- "You don't have to earn the right to rest"

**Why it works:** Taps into guilt/shame, offers relief, feels like a friend giving permission

---

### Formula 3: VULNERABLE CONFESSION (Relatability)
- "I Googled 'is my baby broken' at 3am"
- "The moment I realized I was burned out"
- "I thought self-care was selfish (I was wrong)"
- "The lie I believed about good parenting"
- "Why I stopped following rigid routines"

**Why it works:** Vulnerability builds connection, "me too" factor, humanizes expert

---

### Formula 4: DATA/INSIGHT (Authority + Curiosity)
- "The research everyone ignores about parental sleep"
- "What happens to your brain when you're sleep-deprived"
- "3 studies that changed how I think about rest"
- "The surprising link between your sleep and baby's behavior"
- "Science says: Your rest isn't selfish"

**Why it works:** Authority positioning, promises new information, intellectually engaging

---

### Formula 5: DIRECT BENEFIT (Clear Value Proposition)
- "5 ways to protect your sleep (without sleep training)"
- "How to rest AND respond to your baby"
- "Build sleep rhythms that actually work for your life"
- "The both/and approach to gentle parenting"
- "Before you burn out: Do these 3 things"

**Why it works:** Clear value, actionable, solution-focused, benefit-driven

---

### BONUS: Formula 6: TIME-SENSITIVE / URGENCY (Use Sparingly)
- "Before you hit burnout, read this"
- "Don't wait until you break"
- "You have 24 hours to grab this free guide"
- "Last chance: Early access closes tonight"

**Why it works:** Creates urgency, drives opens, but use sparingly to maintain trust

---

### Subject Line A/B Testing Strategy:

**Test Variables:**
- Question vs. Statement
- Emotional vs. Rational
- Personal (you/your) vs. General
- Short (under 40 chars) vs. Longer (40-60 chars)
- Emoji vs. No Emoji

**Track:**
- Open rates by formula type
- Click rates by formula type
- Reply rates (engagement depth)

**Optimize:**
- Lead with top 2 performing formulas
- Rotate to prevent fatigue
- Match subject line tone to email content (trust-building)
